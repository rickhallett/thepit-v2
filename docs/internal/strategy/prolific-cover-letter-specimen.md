I'm looking for a product engineering role where the work shapes AI development at a systemic level. Prolific sits at exactly that layer - the human data that determines whether models reflect reality or reflect their training artefacts.

Portfolio at oceanheart.ai/portfolio. Public repos: github.com/rickhallett/thepit, github.com/rickhallett/thepit-v2.

Richard

## Slop Reduction Analysis — What's Working

| Factor | Example | Why it reads human |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| No tally voice | Doesn't say "5 years experience across 3 domains" | Talks about what was done, not inventories |
| Specific vocabulary | "sycophantic drift, epistemic theatre" | Domain-specific terms a generalist wouldn't reach for |
| Asymmetric structure | Para 1 = thesis hook, Para 2 = stack, Para 3 = CBT transfer, Para 4 = positioning | Not balanced "intro-body-body-conclusion" template |
| No superlatives | Doesn't say "passionate", "excited", "thrilled" | Confident but not performative |
| Concrete artifacts | "pitkeel", "gauntlet", named repos | Verifiable specifics, not abstract claims |
| First-person stance | "I've spent", "I'm looking for" | Not hedged with "one might" or passive voice |
| No redundant antithesis | Doesn't say "not just X, but Y" repeatedly | Single clean contrast in opener, then moves on |
| Missing expected beats | No "I would be a great fit" or "I look forward to" | Absence of boilerplate signals authenticity |
| Short sentences in key positions | "The domain transfer is direct." | Punchy close to paragraph, not run-on |
| Honest framing | "What might be useful context" | Not overselling the CBT connection |

**Core principle:** Specificity + restraint. Say the concrete thing, then stop.
